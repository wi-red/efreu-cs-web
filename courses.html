Courses & Training Programs

An overview of the Bachelor, Engineering and Master tracks offered by the Computer Science Department.

Our programs

Three complementary tracks

Whether you join us straight after high school or after a two-year preparatory program, we offer a coherent path from the first year up to the engineering degree, with the option to continue with a research-oriented Master.

Bachelor in Computer Science

3 years
Open to high school graduates. Provides a solid foundation in programming, algorithms, web development and databases.

Engineering Program

5 years
Recognised by the French CTI commission. Combines a generalist engineering core with a specialisation chosen in the fourth year.

Master in Computer Science

2 years
Research-oriented track open after the Bachelor. Tracks in AI, cybersecurity, data science and software engineering.

Engineering program

Specialisations (4th and 5th year)

Students who continue into the Engineering program choose a major specialisation at the start of the fourth year. All specialisations include a long-form research or industrial project and a six-month end-of-studies internship.

Software Engineering

Distributed systems, software architecture, DevOps, cloud computing, agile development methods, code quality and continuous integration.

Data Science & AI

Statistical learning, deep learning, natural language processing, computer vision, big data infrastructures, ethics of AI.

Cybersecurity

Cryptography, network security, ethical hacking, security audit and pentesting, secure software development, governance and risk management.

Networks & Cloud

Advanced TCP/IP, software-defined networks, network virtualisation, cloud-native applications, telecoms infrastructure.

Embedded Systems & IoT

Real-time operating systems, embedded C, hardware-software co-design, sensor networks, low-power architectures.
